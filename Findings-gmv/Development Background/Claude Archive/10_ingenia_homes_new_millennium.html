TITLE: Homes for the New Millennium: New Technology and Innovation at Greenwich Millennium Village

PUBLICATION: Ingenia Magazine, Issue 8, 2001

AUTHORS: Robert Hawley (Chairman, Taylor Woodrow PLC) and Richard Hodkinson (Innovation Consultant)



SUMMARY:

GMV is an innovative mixed-use residential development on the eastern side of Greenwich Peninsula adjacent to the River Thames. 1,377 homes, 20% affordable/social housing integrated with private housing. Supplemented by commercial and retail space. All homes linked through an IT network with internal data cabling.



Developer: Taylor Woodrow Capital Developments and Countryside Properties joint venture (Greenwich Millennium Village Ltd).

Project value: approximately £250 million to be built over 6 years.

Construction commenced: late 1999. First houses occupied: December 2000.



TECHNICAL INNOVATION TARGETS:

- Construction waste: reduce by 50%

- Primary energy: reduce by 80% (includes CHP, improved insulation, better heating controls — achieves 65% reduction in practice)

- Water consumption: targeted reduction of up to 30%

- Construction costs and project duration: targeted reductions of up to 30%



ENERGY:

- GMV was the first major UK private housing development to incorporate Combined Heat and Power (CHP)

- CHP facilities generate electricity locally; associated heat serves hot water and heating demand

- Village-wide community heating system — metered at each dwelling

- CO2 reduction of ~67% versus all-electric benchmark

- Phase 2 energy centre sized to meet total energy demand for 550 dwellings in that phase



STRUCTURE:

- Concrete framed construction for high-rise apartments

- Partial use of cement replacement concrete; wide-scale use for high-rise frames proved unviable due to early age strength gain constraints

- Off-site construction attempted but challenging to achieve cost-effectively at targeted standards

- International sourcing of products; emphasis on standardisation across whole project



ENVIRONMENTAL DESIGN:

- Passive-solar orientation

- Large south-facing glazing

- External shading via balconies/canopies

- High-grade insulation

- Efficient appliances

- Car parking restricted and sited away from properties

- Secure bicycle storage

- Ecology park and landscaped public spaces



RETROSPECTIVE (Ingenia Issue 41, c.2009 — "Millennium Homes Revisited"):

- EcoHomes 'Excellent' rating achieved

- Lessons learned from running CHP system for 8+ years

- Two different CHP solutions incorporated for phases 1 and 2

- CO2 reduction confirmed at 67% vs all-electric benchmark

- Note: important lessons learned over the 8 years the system had been running



NOTE ON STRUCTURE DETAIL (from Ingenia revisit):

"To create the high residential density for Greenwich Millennium Village, concrete framed construction was used for the high rise apartments."
