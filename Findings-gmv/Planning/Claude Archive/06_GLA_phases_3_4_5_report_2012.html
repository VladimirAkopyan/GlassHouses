PLANNING APPLICATION DETAILS:

Application type: Hybrid (part detailed, part outline)

Permission granted: 30 March 2012

Reference: 12/0022/O (Royal Borough of Greenwich)



DETAILED ELEMENT (Parcel 1): 459 residential units + energy centre, open space, highways/transport works and ancillary works



OUTLINE ELEMENT (Parcels 2-5):

- 1,746 residential units

- 6,902 sqm non-residential floorspace (A1-A4 / B1 / D1-D2 uses)

- Up to two energy centres

- Open space, highways and transport works



DESIGN:

- Architects Jestico + Whiles led design work, working with Peter Barber Architects and Studio 54 Architecture

- One tall building: 20 storeys high

- Lower-rise buildings: 3–5 storeys

- "Human-scale" scheme mixing townhouses and apartments

- Contemporary, high-end living spaces rated Code Level Four for sustainable homes



ENERGY & SUSTAINABILITY:

- Combined heat and power (gas-fired and biomass/biofuel)

- Greywater recycling and rainwater harvesting

- Double and quadruple glazing

- High levels of thermal insulation and air tightness

- Acoustic consideration: Long 10-storey apartment blocks used as noise barriers against adjacent wharf/aggregate operations

- Large glass screens placed between barrier buildings to allow light while blocking noise (acoustic consultants Sandy Brown)



GLA OUTCOME:

Mayor content for council to determine, subject to conditions and S106 agreement.

Design Council CABE and the GLA endorsed the plans.



RESIDENT ASSOCIATION CONCERNS (raised at time of 2012 referral):

- Scale of development

- Ecology park adjacency

- Density

- Context impacts



SUBSEQUENT AMENDMENTS:

- Two Section 73 applications have varied the consent since 2012

- First S73: varied footprint, number of units, floorspace, elevations and landscaping for Block 107 (the "2014 amendment")

- Second S73: further variations



NOTE: The GMV 500s (Plots 501-503) application in 2025-26 seeks to vary the 2012 hybrid permission via a further Section 73 application. The Planning Board deferred both applications in February/March 2026 after significant resident opposition (loss of consented green space, height/massing, daylight loss, heritage/view impacts).
