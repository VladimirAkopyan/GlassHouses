SECTION 6: EARLY DESIGN DISPUTE — HTA ARCHITECTS vs DEVELOPER (NEW)

===========================================================================



Source: https://www.building.co.uk/news/war-of-words-breaks-out-over-2000-village-failure/4158.article

Building Magazine, c.1999



KEY FINDING: A public dispute between the original design team and the developer broke out 

BEFORE construction was complete. HTA Architects submitted evidence to an official government 

inquiry alleging:



1. Lack of properly resourced project management team with responsibility for managing the 

   process in an integrated way (they cited a Tavistock Institute report from March 1999 

   warning this would "result in disaster")

2. Replacement of every member of the design team except Ralph Erskine, and reduction of 

   Erskine's role — making it impossible to deliver the design vision

3. Failure to develop the whole scheme to RIBA Stage D at the start of the process

4. Failure to implement the Egan agenda for construction management, prefabrication and 

   supply-chain integration

5. Failure to include a mix of uses in each phase



Developer response (Alan Cherry, Countryside Properties chairman): committed to deliver 

standards agreed in legal agreement with English Partnerships and s106 agreement with 

Greenwich Council; said an independent review would confirm they were "well on course."



SIGNIFICANCE: This 1999 dispute predates construction and is the earliest public record of 

governance/delivery concerns at GMV. Combined with the later flooding case (described as 

"death by sub-contracting"), the acoustic complaints of 2005, and the cladding failures of 

the 2020s, it establishes a very long thread of delivery and subcontracting quality concerns 

running through the entire history of the development.

NOTE ON HEAT NETWORK FOR PHASES 3-5:

"The heat network is supplied by a gas CHP-powered energy centre owned and operated by a 

third party." Metropolitan built/operates the distribution network; a separate third party 

owns/operates the energy centre itself.



SUSTAINABILITY MEASURES IN PHASES 3-5 (confirms MVHR):

"Sustainability measures include rainwater harvesting, thermal and acoustic glazing and 

VENTILATION HEAT-RECOVERY SYSTEMS."



KEY FINDING: This is the first explicit confirmation that GMV phases 3-5 (i.e. the East side 

and later development) HAS MVHR ("ventilation heat-recovery systems"), while the Farnsworth 

due-diligence report notes MVHR was NOT confirmed for phases 1-2 (the original Farnsworth 

Court era). This Metropolitan source explicitly confirms the technical split between phases.
