6 Maurer, Metcalfe, Holly & New Becquerel Courts, Faraday, Alamaro, DaVinci & Newton Lodges, Greenwich Millenium Village Phases 1 & 2, West Parkside, Greenwich, SE10 0BY - Ref: 24/3879/F PDF 681 KB

Ward: Greenwich Peninsula

The Planning Board is requested to grant full planning permission for the removal and replacement of combustible materials and build-ups in the existing external cladding materials on buildings within the original phases 1 & 2 of Greenwich Millennium Village (Maurer Court, Metcalfe Court, Faraday Lodge, Alamaro Lodge, Da Vinci Lodge, Newton Lodge, Holly Court & New Becquerel Court).

Decision:

Resolved that full planning permission be granted for the removal and replacement of combustible materials and build-ups in the existing external cladding materials on buildings within the original phases 1 & 2 of Greenwich Millennium Village (Maurer Court, Metcalfe Court, Faraday Lodge, Alamaro Lodge, Da Vinci Lodge, Newton Lodge, Holly Court & New Becquerel Court).

 

Subject to –

·      The Conditions set out in Appendix 2 of the report and its addendum.

·      Amendment to Condition 7 – Materials, that the Sto Render Grain: Strukturmuster 1.0 / be colour matched to the original vibrant colour pallet on the Faraday, Alamaro & Newton blocks (in Phase 2); so as to maintain the original architectural design ideal of these iconic blocks.

·      The following additional Informative:

Informative 2

‘In respect of the Greenwich Millenium Village blocks Faraday Lodge, Alamaro Lodge & Maurer Court only, the applicant and the management company are advised to engage in discussions with residents to conduct an investigation into the feasibility of the cladding product Parklex Prodema GRCLAD-B as a potential alternative replacement material for the existing Cedar Wood cladding as suggested by residents.’  

 

That the Assistant Director (Planning & Building Control) be authorised to make any minor changes to the detailed wording of the recommended conditions as set out in Appendix 2 of the report, its addendum and the minutes of this Planning Board meeting, where the Assistant Director (Planning & Building Control) considers it appropriate, before issuing the final decision notice.

Minutes:

The Planning Board accepted an illustrative presentation of the application from the Planning Officer who informed Members that the existing rendered and timber cladding as well as other elements of the external fenestration materials do not meet the current Building Regulations in terms of combustibility and fire safety and therefore needed to be replaced.

 

The Planning Officer advised members that the coloured render cladding, installed in 2000, had faded over time. The applicant proposed using similarly muted colours to preserve the current character and avoid a polarising appearance. If Members preferred a more vibrant colour palette, it would need to be applied uniformly across the entire development rather than selectively, to maintain visual consistency. Feedback from the pre-application process indicated that residents expected the new appearance to closely match the existing tones.

 

Regarding the timber cladding, Planning Officers were advised that no real timber products met the fire safety regulations and technical standards. Similar developments across London, facing the same issue, were using a fibre cement board as a compliant alternative, similar to that proposed.  It was acknowledged that the fire safety remedial works would cause some disruption to residents. However, a construction management plan, secured by planning condition, would include measures to mitigate impacts such as scaffolding, noise, vibration, and dust.

 

The Planning Board heard from five residents, including the Leaseholders’ Representative, who expressed appreciation for the developer’s efforts. The speakers’ focused on the three lake-facing blocks; Alamaro Lodge, Faraday Lodge, and Maurer Court, part of the original Ralph Erskine scheme known for its vibrant colours and distinctive design, with the speakers supporting the use of the original red ochre, orange, and black palette,  rather than emulating the faded colours, noting that the vibrant colours, dome roofs, and cedar cladding were all features that made Greenwich Millennium Village distinctive and were of recognised iconic design and image.

 

Whilst accepting that the cedar wood cladding had to be replaced, it was felt that the proposed replacement cladding was too dark, dull, and plasticky, did not mimic a natural finish and would be detracting from the architectural vision.  Residents sought solution that would maintain the original vision of the buildings, which, it was felt, the proposed composite boards would not. 

 

A speaker advocated alternative materials such as Corten steel or fire-safe timber be considered to better reflect the original aesthetic.  They emphasized the role as custodians of the site’s legacy, and that residents were not seeking enhancement, and called for the application to be split—approving Phases 1 and 3, which faced no objections, while reconsidering Phase 2, which submitted the greater level of objections.

 

Speakers requested that, given works on these blocks are not scheduled until late 2026, residents requested a planning condition be added to explore the use of more suitable materials that would provide a greater like-for-like replacement in terms of the visual aesthetic.
